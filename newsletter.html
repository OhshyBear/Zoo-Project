Shy Bear Zoo's March News Letter

Aiptasia Removal

This months heavy maintenaince focus will be the removal of Aiptasia from all of our tanks. Most aquarium goers are unaware that this anemone is actually considered a pest in an aquarium setting as it can sting and burn the fish and other life in the aquarium. For our predator tanks we will be removing it chemically but did you know that Aiptasia has natural predators as well. So for the tanks without puffer fish octopus and eels we will be adding peppermint shrimp and aiptasia eating file fish to remedy the situation! Please come out and enjoy the new additions to the aquarium and root them on as they clean up the aquarium.

Red Pandas Have Arrived

It's been several months of development and planning to ensure the habitat was perfect for these little guys but the Red Pandas have finally reached the Shy Bear Zoo. We will honor a week of adjustment before making the exhibit open to the public So come Join us on Friday March 8th to visit these little guys. Exhibit will be located in Asia World as they begin to settle down at the Shy Bear Zoo. These furry friends were rescued from poachers and will not be able to return to the wild due to some extensive injuries. As always Shy Bear Zoo will continue our conservation efforts to keep animals like these from undergoing trauma at the hands of humans.
